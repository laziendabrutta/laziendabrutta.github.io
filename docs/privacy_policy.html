3. Purposes & legal bases

  1. Service delivery (exporting posts & images) – Art. 6 (1)(b) GDPR
  2. Technical management & security (logging, abuse prevention) – Art. 6 (1)(f) GDPR
  3. Legal obligations (e.g. security logs) – Art. 6 (1)(c) GDPR

4. How we use & store data

  • Exported data are saved locally on the user’s machine in linkedin_posts.json.
  • No data are shared with third parties except technical processors (cloud/hosting) under Art. 28 GDPR contracts.
  • OAuth tokens are stored only in memory or, if caching is enabled, in an encrypted file on the same host.
  • Retention: data are not kept for more than 30 days unless the user deletes them earlier, matching LinkedIn Marketing API requirements.

5. Data sharing

  • IT providers (hosting/back‑up) – limited access, GDPR Art. 28 contracts
  • Competent authorities – only when required by law or court order

6. International transfers

Servers are located in the EU. If non‑EEA providers are used, appropriate safeguards (standard contractual clauses) are applied.

7. Security

  • TLS 1.2+ encryption for all LinkedIn communications
  • Encrypted storage of OAuth tokens
  • Principle of least privilege (only r_organization_social scope)

8. Data subject rights

Users may exercise their rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability and objection (GDPR Art. 15‑22) by emailing ceo@laziendabrutta.it. They may also lodge a complaint with their local supervisory authority.

9. Cookies & tracking

The desktop/CLI version uses no cookies. If a web UI is offered, only essential session cookies are used; no profiling cookies.
